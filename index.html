Structural concerns specific to a 1860 farmhouse

The combination of 165-year-old structure + fieldstone foundation + Michigan basement is the highest-risk inspection profile in residential. None of the listed renovations address structure. Specific concerns the inspector should look at:

ConcernWhat to look for
Fieldstone foundationMortar deterioration, water infiltration, displaced or rolled stones, parging that may hide cracks. Probe mortar with a pick.
Sill plateWhere wood meets the stone wall — probe with an awl for rot and termite damage. The most common 1860-era structural failure point.
Michigan basement / crawl spaceChronic moisture, mold, no thermal envelope, vapor-barrier integrity in any dirt-floor section, joist condition above.
Balloon framingLook for evidence of no fire-stops between floors, sister joists, beam additions in hidden areas. Check for sagging or sloping floors.
Plumbing supply pipe materialGalvanized steel = old (will need replacement). Copper with lead solder pre-1986 = lead exposure risk. PEX or modern copper = updated.

Items to confirm with each vendor

  • Pillar to Post: confirm scope includes (a) thermal imaging of walls as well as ceilings — moisture-staining behind drywall is a frequent issue in 1860 farmhouses; (b) outlet-tracing to detect abandoned active knob-and-tube; (c) panel inspection for double-tapped breakers, aluminum branch wiring, and Federal Pacific / Zinsco panels; (d) crawl space access and probe; (e) attic walk-through to verify insulation type (especially looking for vermiculite); (f) buried-fuel-tank metal-detector sweep.
  • Roberts Well Drilling — David: confirm whether the well inspection includes a lab-quality water sample (arsenic, lead, coliform, nitrates, iron, manganese, hardness, pH at minimum). If their standard inspection is system-only, arrange a separate water-test through Ingham County Health Department or an independent lab. Arsenic is the priority — don't skip it; the well draws from the Saginaw Formation which is the geology in southern Michigan most associated with elevated arsenic.
  • Wellman Septic (or whichever septic vendor): confirm scope includes opening the tank lid, measuring sludge depth, inspecting the baffles, locating and probing the drain field, and a dye test to confirm the field is functional. Ask for the most recent pumping receipt from the seller.

Specific items the home inspector should open up and probe

Foundation & basement

  • Probe mortar between fieldstones with a pick — check for crumbling, missing mortar.
  • Look for water staining, mineral efflorescence, prior parging that may hide cracks.
  • Sill plate (where wood meets stone): probe with an awl for rot, termite damage.
  • Posts/columns supporting first floor — confirm proper bearing and footings.
  • Vapor barrier in any dirt-floor crawl space.
  • Look up at floor joists from below for sagging, sister-joists, beam additions.
  • Confirm exterior grade and downspout discharge — does water drain away from foundation?

Framing

  • Evidence of balloon framing without fire blocks (peek into wall voids if possible during electrical/plumbing inspection).
  • Bouncy / sloping floors.
  • Joist notching/drilling for utility runs (1990s+ rough-ins are a frequent code issue).
  • Roof framing in attic — visible water stains, sister rafters, sagging ridge.

Plumbing

  • Identify supply pipe material throughout: galvanized steel (old) vs copper with lead solder pre-1986 vs PEX or modern copper (updated).
  • Check for active leaks under all fixtures and at the well-line entry.
  • Water pressure at multiple fixtures simultaneously.
  • Drain wastes — cast iron vs PVC vs ABS.
  • Hot water heater age, type (electric/propane/oil), pan/drain.
  • Confirm shutoffs work at every fixture.

Electrical

  • Open the panel — confirm proper bonding/grounding, no double-tapped breakers, no aluminum branch wiring, no Federal Pacific or Zinsco panels.
  • Trace at least 2-3 random outlets back to confirm no abandoned active knob-and-tube.
  • AFCI/GFCI in required locations (kitchens, baths, basement, exterior, garage, laundry).
  • Smoke / CO detectors hardwired (claimed by listing — verify).
  • Service amperage adequate (100A min, 200A preferred).

HVAC

  • New furnace: confirm it was permitted (call township per Permits). Get manufacturer + serial #, AHRI certificate, warranty paperwork.
  • Have inspector look at supply/return ducts — old farmhouse ducts are often undersized or leaky.
  • Combustion air supply, venting, condensate handling.
  • AC system if present — age, refrigerant type, condition.
  • Backup heat plan (in case furnace goes down in MI winter).

Roof — extra scrutiny given the seller-entity history

  • Type (standing seam vs exposed-fastener metal).
  • Underlayment (synthetic vs felt; ice-and-water shield at eaves and valleys).
  • Flashing at chimney, plumbing penetrations, valleys, sidewalls.
  • Snow guards (Michigan winter requirement).
  • Manufacturer's installation instructions followed (the 2019 LARA case specifically alleged Snyder did NOT follow manufacturer instructions on a metal roof).
  • Manufacturer warranty paperwork and registration.
  • Permit number and final inspection sign-off.

Other

  • Windows: confirm which were actually replaced vs. left original. Lead-paint glazing on remaining originals.
  • Vinyl siding: j-channels, flashings, weep holes. Original siding underneath (asbestos shingles, cedar, etc.).
  • Foundation grade and drainage.
  • Outbuildings — including detached garage; wiring is often unpermitted retrofits.
  • Driveway encroachments, shared easements, right-of-way clarity.
  • Chimney(s): scope the flue if wood-burning is intended.

Questions to ask the seller — in writing, before contingency expiry

Required disclosures (legally mandated)

  1. Provide the Federal Lead-Based Paint Disclosure form (required for pre-1978 sales).
  2. Provide the Michigan Seller's Disclosure Statement in full, with all known defects.

Permits — call out by name; demand permit numbers and inspection sign-offs

  1. Was the furnace replacement permitted by Stockbridge Township? Permit number and inspection sign-off.
  2. Was the roof replacement permitted? Permit number, contractor name, and inspection sign-off. Provide the roofing manufacturer name and warranty registration.
  3. Were the electrical updates permitted? Permit number(s), contractor name(s), final inspection.
  4. Were the plumbing updates permitted? Permit number(s), contractor name(s), final inspection.
  5. Was any structural repair to foundation, sills, or framing done? Permit, contractor, photos.

Systems and services

  1. Provide the most recent septic tank pumping receipt and date of last drain-field service.
  2. Provide any water-quality test results (arsenic, bacteria, nitrates, lead) from the past 5 years.
  3. Has the well pump been replaced since the well was drilled in 1980? When? Provide service records.
  4. When was the pressure tank last replaced?
  5. Was any old underground storage tank (heating oil, propane, gasoline) ever used or removed on the property? Closure documentation if so.

Hazards and hidden conditions

  1. Knob-and-tube wiring — was any found and removed during the renovation? Where? Provide receipts. (This affects insurability.)
  2. Is there any vermiculite insulation present, or was it removed during the renovation? Provide abatement documentation if removed.
  3. Any asbestos-containing materials identified during renovation (floor tile, pipe wrap, plaster, boiler wrap, siding shingles)?
  4. Any lead pipes or lead solder in plumbing supply lines?
  5. Any mold remediation done in the basement / crawl space?

Title and tax

  1. Confirm payoff statement for mortgage 2022-032386 ($67,500 to Hawkins Trust) and that it will be released at closing.
  2. Confirm the 2025 tax foreclosure affecting this parcel was redeemed — provide a copy of the redemption certificate that names parcel 33-16-16-04-400-023 specifically (not a "multiple legals" group document).
  3. Are there any easements, mineral-rights leases, oil-and-gas leases, or surface-use agreements on this parcel?

Other
